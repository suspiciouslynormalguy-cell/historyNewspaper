How a Shockingly Brutal Attack from Japanese Bombers has Thrown America into Turmoil

The bombing of Pearl harbor has raised many questions including "Will America choose to fight?"

Updated 2 days ago at 11:59 PST

Article by Douglass Mac Dougle

HONOLULU - December 2, 1941: The citizens of the island of Oahu woke up to the sounds of explosions and gunfire. 2,403 lives were lost to the bombings. The unprovoked attack from the Japanese government surprised and shocked many who thought they were safe due to our isolationist policy, with some stating, “bombs fell from the sky and we were none the wiser.”

Initial reports confirm heavy casualties, with at least 2,403 lives lost and significant damage to military installations and naval vessels. Due to this, our government is expected to change this policy later this month, with many gearing towards war with the Japanese.

With many angry over the turn of events and the destruction of our fleet and our president stating that this is “a day that will live in infamy”, it is becoming more likely that we will face the same destruction that France and Britain already have.

Nevertheless, with fascist powers like Germany and Italy aiming to take over most of Europe, the Allies need more support from the West.
