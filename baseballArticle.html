From the Ballpark to the Battlefield: How the National Pastime Is Powering American Morale

Baseball, a sport that's truly American in nature has been boosting morale and brightening spirits during the tension of WW2.

Updated 2 days ago at 11:59 PST

Article by Douglass Mac Dougle

While the rest of the world is fighting a war on several fronts, America is fighting a war on the baseball field. Even as players get enlisted into the war, America’s passion for the sport hasn’t faded.

With even president Franklin D Roosevelt encouraging our citizens to play, citing the sport as “thoroughly worthwhile”, the lower quality of play with people not fit to serve in the war hasn’t stopped avid watchers.

However, the lower quality of play has caused the negro league to blow up with traction it hasn’t seen for a while. With players like Jackie Robinson taking center stage, it’s hard not to see how baseball was the sport America needed.
