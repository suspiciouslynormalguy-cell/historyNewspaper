Typhoon Cobra Cripples Ships near the Phillipine Coast

In the heart of the Pacific, three of our ships, USS Hull, USS Monaghan, and USS Spence, faced increadible winds and rain.

Updated 2 days ago at 11:59 PST

Article by Douglass Mac Dougle

While our brave troops are still fighting on the front lines, another enemy has been silently sneaking up on them from behind, impacting troop morale and even impacting bombing raids. Just last week, a storm flooded the waters surrounding the Philippines Archipelago and sank three of our destroyers, the USS Hull, the USS Monaghan, and the USS Spence.

We are now calling this storm the Typhoon Cobra, a deadly cyclone in a sequence of typhoons that have been wreaking havoc around that area. We sincerely wish our military luck in wrangling this particular storm and hope they can return home soon. Our critical work of securing the Philippines cannot wait.

Our time for isolationism has to come to an end, as we are needed to stop Hitler in his conquest across Europe.
